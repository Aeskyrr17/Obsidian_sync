Using the formula

$$

T = 2 \pi \sqrt{(\frac{I}{mgd})} \\

$$

- T is the measured period

- L is the length of the bar

- x is the distance from the pivot to the center of mass

- g is the gravitatioanl acceleration

  

$$

g = \frac{4\pi^2I}{mdT^2}\\

I = \frac{1}{12}L^2+x^2

$$

Hence,

$$

g = \frac{2\pi^2(L^2/12+x^2)}{T^2x}

$$

$$

L = 280.07mm = 0.2887m

$$

From the fitting result, the value of g is

$$

g = 9.63 m/s^2

$$

  

The relatice error with respect to the real value $g_{real}$ = 9.80m/s^2 is

$$

\text{Relativ error} = |\frac{g_{cal}-g_{real}}{g_{real}}|\times 100\% \approx 1.76\%

$$

  

Overall, the experimental result for gravitational acceleration is $g = 9.63$

The relative error is $1.76 \%$
